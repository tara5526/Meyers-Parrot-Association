Poicephalus Meyeri



The Meyer's parrot, also known as the brown parrot, is a native bird of Africa that can be found in woodlands and savannas, graphed below in blue.



A Meyer's parrot has grey to black feathers, green belly, blue back, and bright yellow feathers on the front of the wings. When a bird reaches the age of around 1 year, it will start to get additional yellow markings on the top of its head.

They are around 8.2 inches in length, and typically weigh between 3.4 and 4.7 ounces.

Meyer's parrots are affectionate, loving birds with a stable temperament, and are relatively easy (and highly rewarding) to have a as a pet. They are highly intelligent and can learn many words, sounds and tricks. Meyer's Parrot Association sells an ebook that describes and evaluates the best training methods for these incredible birds.
